Kevin Hart was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, on July 6th, 1979. He was primary raised by his mother, Nancy Hart. His father was Henry Witherspoon, and he had a cocaine addiction and spent most of Kevin's childhood in and out of jail. Kevin graduated from George Washington High School and then attended Community College in Philadelphia but eventually dropped out and moved to New York City. Kevin briefly worked as a shoe salesman in Brocton, Massachusetts, before realizing that he wanted to pursue stand-up comedy.

Kevin first started stand-up and the Laff House in Philadelphia. Kevin did not use his real name in his early performances. Kevin went by a stage name called Lil Kev. His early stand-up career wasn't very successful. He was booed off the stage many times when he first started. Eventually, Kevin developed his own unique style. He tried to imitate another comedian, Chris Tucker but realized he had his own rhythm by going into his own life and experiences when doing his performances.
